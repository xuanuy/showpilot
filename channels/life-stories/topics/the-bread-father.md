## Script

Every evening, a little boy waited by the door with his small dog, listening for footsteps. No matter how heavy the day had been, his father always walked in with the same gentle smile. One night the boy asked him why he never seemed angry. His father knelt down and said, the happiest children grow up in homes where no one keeps score. Where we forgive quickly, and never blame each other for small things. Years later, that boy became a father too, and he carried the same quiet smile through his own front door. Because the warmth of a home is not built from money. It is built from how we choose to treat the people waiting for us. What did your home teach you about love? Tell me below.
