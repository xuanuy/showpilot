## Script

A poor boy found a wallet on a busy street, thick with cash, more money than his family had seen in months. His hands shook. He could have kept it and no one would ever know. Instead he spent an hour tracking down the address inside and returned it untouched. The wealthy owner, suspicious, counted every bill in front of him, then asked coldly why he hadn't taken any. The boy said simply, because then I would have to live as someone who steals, and I would rather be poor than be that. The man went silent. The next morning he offered the boy a job, then paid for his schooling for years. Your character is the one thing poverty can never take, and the one thing money can never buy. Would you have returned it? Be honest below.
