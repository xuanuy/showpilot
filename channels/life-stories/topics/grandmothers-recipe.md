## Script

A young woman finally inherited her grandmother's famous soup recipe, the one the whole family begged for at every holiday. But the card had a strange instruction near the end: add one spoonful of nothing, and stir slowly while you think of someone you love. She thought it was a mistake, an old woman's confusion. So she made the soup exactly right, perfect ingredients, perfect timing. It never tasted the same. Years later she understood. The spoonful of nothing was the pause. The slowing down. The love stirred in on purpose. Her grandmother wasn't writing a recipe, she was teaching her how to cook for people, not just feed them. Some lessons hide inside the things we almost throw away.
