## Script

Two neighbors planted the same trees on the same day. The first watered his constantly, checked the roots, dug them up to see if they were growing, and worried over every leaf. The second watered his well, then mostly left them alone, trusting the roots to do their quiet work underground. A storm came years later. The first man's trees, with their shallow anxious roots, toppled over instantly. The second man's trees stood firm, because being left to struggle a little had forced their roots to grow deep and strong. People are the same. Constant rescuing keeps roots shallow. Sometimes the kindest thing is to let someone grow through a little hardship. Are you growing roots, or just chasing comfort? Tell me what you think below.
