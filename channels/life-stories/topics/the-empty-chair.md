## Script

A neighbor noticed that the old man next door set two plates at dinner every single night, though he clearly lived alone. Curious and a little worried, she finally knocked and asked about it. He smiled gently. My wife and I ate dinner together for fifty one years, he said. She always told me the day I stop setting her place is the day I start forgetting her. So I keep her seat. Not out of sadness, but out of gratitude. The neighbor went home and looked at her own rushed dinners, her family on their phones, barely speaking. That night she put the phones away and asked everyone how their day really went. Grief taught a stranger what comfort had made her forget. Who do you keep a place for in your heart? Tell me below.
