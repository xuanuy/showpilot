## Script

Every morning an old man came to the same little coffee shop, ordered the cheapest cup, and left a tip three times bigger than the coffee. The young barista never understood why. One quiet morning she finally asked him. He smiled and said, when I was young and had nothing, a stranger paid for my breakfast and told me to pass it on someday. I have been passing it on for forty years. The next week the old man stopped coming. He had passed away. But the barista kept a jar by the register, quietly paying for the coffee of anyone who looked like they were having a hard day. Kindness never really ends. It just changes hands. Who passed their kindness on to you? Tell me their story below.
