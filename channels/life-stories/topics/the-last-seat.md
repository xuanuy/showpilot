## Script

On the same evening train, a tired man in a work uniform gave up his seat every single day, to mothers, to the elderly, to anyone who looked more worn out than him. A college student watched this happen for months and finally asked why he never just rested after such long shifts. The man smiled and said, my father stood his whole life so I could sit. Standing a little is how I thank him. The student never forgot it. Years later, riding that same train, she found herself offering her seat to a stranger without even thinking. Some kindness isn't taught with words. It's just quietly passed from one tired person to the next. Who taught you how to be kind without ever lecturing you? Share their story below.
