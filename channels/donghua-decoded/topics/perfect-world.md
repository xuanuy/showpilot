## Script

Perfect World is hundreds of episodes long and people still binge the whole thing. Why? Because Shi Hao is the rare cultivation hero who actually loses things. Yes, he's born with monstrous talent, but the world keeps taking from him: his home, the people he loves, the time he'll never get back. Most xianxia is about climbing. Perfect World is about what the climb costs you. The animation has turned genuinely cinematic in the recent arcs, but the reason it sticks with you is grief. Shi Hao doesn't fight to become a god, he fights because the alternative is watching everyone he cares about disappear. That emotional weight is what separates a great donghua from a forgettable power fantasy. So tell me honestly: is Perfect World the most underrated long-runner in the genre right now?
