## Script

Shi Hao's story might be the saddest in all of cultivation, and most people only remember the fights. Here's the part that actually matters. He's born a once-in-an-era genius, in a village that genuinely loves him, and the world spends the entire story slowly taking that away. His people, his childhood, his first love, the time he can never get back, all of it sacrificed on the climb to the top. Perfect World tricks you. It looks like a power fantasy, but it's really about grief, about how being the chosen one means outliving everyone who made you who you are. By the time Shi Hao reaches the summit, you finally understand: the real price of immortality is loneliness. Does power ever feel worth that cost? Tell me honestly what you think.
